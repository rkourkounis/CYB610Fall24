Idaho

Idaho’s data breach laws, primarily outlined in Idaho Code §§ 28-51-104 to 28-51-107, require businesses and government entities to notify affected individuals if their personal information is compromised. The notification must be made within 45 days of discovering the breach. If the breach affects more than 1,000 residents, the entity must also notify the Idaho Attorney General. The law mandates that businesses implement reasonable security measures to protect personal information and provide detailed notice to affected individuals, including the type of information involved and steps being taken to address the breach. Non-compliance can result in civil penalties of up to $2,000 per violation.
