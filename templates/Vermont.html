Vermont

Vermont’s data breach notification laws, outlined in the Security Breach Notice Act (9 V.S.A. §§ 2430, 2435), require businesses and state agencies to notify affected individuals and the Vermont Attorney General within 14 days of discovering a breach. Notification must be made without unreasonable delay and no later than 45 days after discovery. The notification must include a description of the breach, the types of personal information involved, and any remediation services offered. If the breach affects more than 1,000 residents, businesses must also notify major credit reporting agencies. The law emphasizes prompt and transparent communication to mitigate the impact of data breaches.
